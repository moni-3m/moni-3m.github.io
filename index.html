I am currently a GIS masters student at Clark University. This is a private research university in Worcester, Massachusetts.
Founded in 1887 with a large endowment from its namesake Jonas Gilman Clark, a prominent businessman, Clark was one of the first modern research universities in the United States.
I am currently part of a research project looking at water crisis in a central region of Mexico, considering climate justice and making use of geospatial open data.



Interests


I have a strong interest in water quality and its sustainable management, as I believe it plays a crucial role in maintaining healthy ecosystems and supporting communities. At this point, I'm especially eager to be involved in projects that focus on the conservation and restoration of natural resources and biodiversity. Contributing to efforts that protect and revive ecosystems, while also promoting balance between human needs and nature, is something I'm very passionate about.


Outside of my academic and research pursuits, I am deeply passionate about sports, with a particular love for horse riding.
